OFAC Enforcement Totals

$266M
Total OFAC penalties and settlements in 2025
Total penalties and settlements in 2025 exceeded $266 million, compared with approximately $49 million in 2024 — a 5.4x increase. Over $215 million came from a single penalty notice issued in December 2025.
Source: Sidley Austin, Five Key Takeaways From 2025 U.S. Sanctions Enforcement
APA: Sidley Austin. (2026). Five Key Takeaways From 2025 U.S. Sanctions Enforcement. https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2026/02/five-key-takeaways-from-2025-us-sanctions-enforcement
$282.7M
OFAC penalties year-to-date June 2026
As of June 1, 2026, OFAC had issued 5 enforcement actions totaling $282.7 million in combined penalties and settlements — already exceeding the full-year 2025 total by June.
Source: OFAC Civil Penalties and Enforcement Information, 2026
APA: OFAC. (2026). Civil Penalties and Enforcement Information. https://ofac.treasury.gov/civil-penalties-and-enforcement-information
$215M
Largest single OFAC penalty in 2025
The largest single OFAC penalty notice in 2025 was $215 million, representing over 80% of total annual enforcement. This reflects a trend toward fewer but significantly larger penalty actions.
Source: OFAC Civil Penalties and Enforcement Information, 2025
APA: OFAC. (2025). 2025 Enforcement Information. https://ofac.treasury.gov/civil-penalties-and-enforcement-information/2025-enforcement-information

Enforcement Trends

5.4x
Year-over-year increase in OFAC penalties
OFAC penalties increased 5.4x from 2024 ($49M) to 2025 ($266M), driven by Treasury's expanded enforcement capacity and new sanctions programs targeting digital assets and AI-related entities.
Source: Corporate Compliance Insights, State of OFAC Sanctions Enforcement 2025-26
APA: Corporate Compliance Insights. (2026). The State of OFAC Sanctions Enforcement in 2025-26. https://www.corporatecomplianceinsights.com/state-ofac-sanctions-enforcement-2026/
5
OFAC enforcement actions YTD 2026
OFAC has issued 5 enforcement actions year-to-date as of June 1, 2026, with individual penalties ranging from $1.1 million to $3.77 million for the smaller cases, plus one major action exceeding $250M.
Source: OFAC Civil Penalties and Enforcement Information, 2026
APA: OFAC. (2026). Civil Penalties and Enforcement Information.

Penalty Ranges

$1.1M – $3.77M
Typical per-case penalty range in 2026
Recent 2026 enforcement actions have resulted in penalties ranging from $1.1 million to $3.77 million per case. Even unknowing violations can result in significant civil penalties.
Source: Sanctions Lawyers Network, OFAC Violation Penalties 2026
APA: Sanctions Lawyers Network. (2026). OFAC Violation Penalties 2026. https://sanctionslawyers.net/blog-en/penalties-for-ofac-violations/
$368,236
Maximum civil penalty per violation (2026)
The maximum civil penalty per OFAC violation is $368,236 (adjusted for inflation in 2026), or twice the transaction value, whichever is greater. Criminal penalties can reach $1M and 20 years imprisonment.
Source: OFAC Enforcement Guidelines
APA: OFAC. (2026). Economic Sanctions Enforcement Guidelines. 31 CFR Part 501.

SDN List

12,000+
Entities and individuals on the SDN List
The Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List contains over 12,000 entities and individuals across more than 35 sanctions programs. The list grows by an average of 800–1,200 entries per year.
Source: OFAC Specially Designated Nationals List
APA: OFAC. (2026). Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List.
35+
Active OFAC sanctions programs
OFAC administers and enforces over 35 active sanctions programs targeting countries, regimes, terrorists, narcotics traffickers, and cyber actors. New programs added in 2025-26 target AI-enabled threats and digital asset networks.
Source: OFAC Sanctions Programs and Country Information
APA: OFAC. (2026). Sanctions Programs and Country Information. https://ofac.treasury.gov/sanctions-programs-and-country-information

Compliance Costs

$4M – $8M
Average annual sanctions compliance cost (mid-size FI)
Mid-size financial institutions spend an estimated $4M–$8M annually on sanctions compliance, including screening technology, staff, training, and independent audits. AI-based screening tools can reduce this by 30–50%.
Source: Corporate Compliance Insights, Industry Survey 2025
APA: Corporate Compliance Insights. (2025). Sanctions Compliance Cost Survey.
30–50%
Cost reduction from AI-based screening
AI-based sanctions screening tools reduce compliance costs by 30–50% for mid-size institutions, primarily through lower false-positive rates (from ~95% to ~60%) and reduced manual review hours.
Source: Industry analysis, sanctions compliance technology providers
APA: SanctionsAI. (2026). Sanctions Screening Efficiency Analysis.

False Positives

~95%
False positive rate in traditional screening
Traditional name-based sanctions screening generates false positive rates of approximately 95%, meaning only 1 in 20 alerts represents a true match. AI-based screening with entity resolution reduces this to ~60% false positives.
Source: Financial Crime Compliance Industry Benchmarks, 2025
APA: FCC Industry Benchmarks. (2025). Sanctions Screening Efficiency Report.

Digital Assets

37%
OFAC actions involving crypto (2025)
Approximately 37% of OFAC's 2025 enforcement actions and designations involved cryptocurrency or digital asset entities, up from 22% in 2024 — reflecting Treasury's increasing focus on the digital asset ecosystem.
Source: Sidley Austin, Five Key Takeaways
APA: Sidley Austin. (2026). Five Key Takeaways From 2025 U.S. Sanctions Enforcement.
$1.4B
Crypto-related sanctions penalties 2025
Crypto-related sanctions penalties totaled an estimated $1.4 billion in 2025 (including the $215M OFAC penalty plus DOJ/FINCEN actions), making digital assets the highest-risk sanctions compliance domain.
Source: Chainalysis, 2026 Crypto Compliance Report
APA: Chainalysis. (2026). 2026 Crypto Compliance Report.

Voluntary Self-Disclosure

50%
Penalty reduction from voluntary self-disclosure
OFAC guidelines provide up to 50% reduction in base penalty for voluntary self-disclosure of apparent violations, provided the disclosure is complete, timely, and accompanied by remediation.
Source: OFAC Economic Sanctions Enforcement Guidelines, 31 CFR Part 501
APA: OFAC. (2024). Economic Sanctions Enforcement Guidelines. 31 CFR Part 501, Appendix A.
