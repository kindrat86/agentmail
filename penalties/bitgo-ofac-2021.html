BitGo — $98,307 OFAC Penalty (2021)

BitGo settled with OFAC for 183 apparent violations of multiple sanctions programs.

Penalty amount: $98,307 · Year: 2021 · Enforcer: OFAC (US Treasury)

What happened

BitGo was penalized by the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) in 2021. The case is instructive for any organization handling cross-border payments, crypto transactions, or customer onboarding where sanctioned parties may be present.

Violations cited

  • Processing transactions involving sanctioned addresses
  • Failed to screen users from sanctioned jurisdictions
  • No automated SDN list updates
  • Lack of geolocation controls

Lessons for compliance teams

  • Screen every counterparty wallet address
  • Automated SDN list synchronization (daily)
  • IP and wallet geolocation on every transaction
  • Real-time alerting on potential matches

How this could have been prevented

In nearly every OFAC penalty case, the root cause is the same: inadequate or outdated sanctions screening. The sanctioned parties were on the SDN list at the time — they simply weren't caught. Real-time screening against the current SDN list, with proper name-matching and fuzzy logic, would have flagged most of these transactions before they completed.
