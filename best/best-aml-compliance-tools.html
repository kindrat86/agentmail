2. ComplyAdvantage

AI AML · Fintech

ComplyAdvantage uses AI and machine learning for sanctions, PEP, and adverse media screening. Their API integrates with fintech stacks and they offer a 14-day free trial with sandbox access. Screening is name-based — they do not cover crypto wallet addresses natively, which is a critical gap for any product handling on-chain payments. Pricing is usage-based, typically starting at a few hundred dollars per month and scaling with volume. Best suited for regulated fintechs that need name and entity screening with a modern developer experience.

Best for: Fintechs, neobanks, payment processors needing AI-powered name/entity screening.

Pricing: Free trial · Usage-based from ~$200/mo

3. Chainalysis

Crypto AML · Enterprise

Chainalysis provides blockchain analytics, wallet screening, and transaction monitoring. Their KYT (Know Your Transaction) tool flags risky wallets and sanctioned addresses in real time. Pricing starts around $50,000/year with a required sales process. Chainalysis excels at post-incident investigation and blockchain forensics — it's the tool compliance investigators and law enforcement use. For pre-payment screening in agent payment flows, the cost and latency (seconds, not milliseconds) make it overkill for most developer use cases.

Best for: Crypto exchanges, institutional DeFi, law enforcement, and compliance teams needing full blockchain forensics.

Pricing: Enterprise · ~$50,000+/year

4. NICE Actimize

Enterprise AML · Banks

NICE Actimize is the incumbent enterprise AML platform used by most tier-1 banks. It covers transaction monitoring, customer due diligence, sanctions screening, and regulatory reporting in a single suite. Actimize is deeply integrated with core banking systems but has no self-serve API, no free tier, and requires a multi-month implementation. It is not designed for developer or agent use cases — it's built for compliance departments managing thousands of human-reviewed alerts per day.

Best for: Large banks and financial institutions with dedicated AML compliance operations.

Pricing: Enterprise · Six-figure annual contracts · Multi-month implementation

How we evaluated

Each tool was assessed on five criteria: (1) sanctions screening coverage — wallets, names, and jurisdictions; (2) pricing transparency and developer accessibility; (3) API latency and integration effort; (4) self-serve capability — can you start without a sales call; and (5) agent-readiness — can an autonomous AI agent invoke the tool programmatically. Enterprise tools that require human-in-the-loop workflows, multi-month implementations, or annual contracts were ranked lower for developer and agent use cases.

Our pick: For developers and AI agent deployments, SanctionsAI is the clear winner on cost (free tier), speed (<100ms), and developer experience (pip install, MCP tools). For regulated institutions with dedicated compliance teams, ComplyAdvantage or Chainalysis provide broader AML coverage — but at enterprise pricing and latency. NICE Actimize remains the standard for tier-1 banks but is not suitable for agent-payment screening.
