OFAC Penalty for Crypto Payment to a Sanctioned Wallet

A DeFi protocol's smart contract autonomously routes a stablecoin payment to a wallet address that — unknown to the protocol team — belongs to an entity on the OFAC SDN list. Here's the financial and legal aftermath.

The scenario

A DeFi lending protocol builds a smart contract that automatically distributes yield to liquidity providers. One provider's wallet address was added to the SDN list 3 months ago, but the protocol's screening — which only checked wallets at onboarding — hasn't caught it. Over 6 months, $47,000 in yield is routed to the sanctioned wallet via 14 separate transactions.

The discovery

A blockchain analytics firm flags the wallet in a routine compliance report to the protocol's banking partner. The bank freezes the protocol's fiat off-ramp account and files a suspicious activity report. The protocol team learns about the issue from their bank, not from their own monitoring.
